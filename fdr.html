Franklin D. Roosevelt

The Dynamic Reformer and Visionary Leader

Born: January 30, 1882

Died: April 12, 1945

Nationality: American

Occupation: Politician, 32nd U.S. President

Notable For: Leading the U.S. through the Great Depression and World War II

Early Life and Education

Franklin Delano Roosevelt was born into a prominent American family in Hyde Park, New York. His early years were shaped by privilege, yet he was driven by a strong sense of duty and compassion for the common man. Educated at Harvard University and later at Columbia Law School (which he did not complete), FDR's formative years laid the foundation for his future political career.
